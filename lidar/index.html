Using LiDAR flown by the state and publicly available, we can generate nearly complete forest inventories without ever having to step foot in the field.

The forest is divided up into 30Ã—30 foot grid cells, and the LiDAR is analyses with state-of-the-art machine learning algorithms. Predictions can then be made of forest inventory attributes within each 30ft cell. Cells can then be added up to generate stand or property level metrics.

These estimates include:

  • Hardwood/softwood percent
  • Mean and maximum tree height
  • Basal area
  • Quadratic mean diameter
  • Tree count
  • Biomass
  • Total stem volume
  • Merchantable stem volume
  • Forest type classification (SW3B, ect..)
